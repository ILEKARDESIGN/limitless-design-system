Budget Expenditure — Projected · Actual · Trajectory
Best for → NDIS plan financial management · Spend pacing · Overrun early warning
The definitive NDIS budget chart showing three critical lines on one view: the dashed white projected (ideal linear pace), solid pink actual spend to date, and dashed pink trajectory showing where current spend rate will land. Yellow dashed ceiling = total plan budget. Cyan vertical = today. Pink-tinted zone above budget line flags projected overrun. This is the chart every SC needs on every participant's dashboard — it answers "are we on track?" instantly.
Design Specifications
Projected: 1.5px dashed white at 35% · Actual: 2.5px solid pink with 5px end dot · Trajectory: 2px dashed pink at 50% · Budget ceiling: 1px dashed yellow at 40% · Today: 1.5px dashed cyan · All labels in accent colours at full opacity · White axis labels at 60% · 6% gridlines

Participant Timeline
Best for → Participant lifecycle view · Key events chronology · Plan period context
Horizontal timeline showing all key events in a participant's journey plotted on a central spine. Events alternate above and below the line to avoid crowding. Colour-coded by event type: pink = plan milestones, cyan = assessments, yellow = agreements, orange = incidents, purple = reviews. Current position marked with a cyan dot. Future events shown as dashed outlines. This gives SCs a complete narrative of the participant's history at a glance.

Risk Forecasting / Risk Trajectory
Best for → Escalating risk detection · Proactive intervention · Compliance forecasting
Time-series showing a participant's composite risk score trending upward through threshold zones. Orange solid for historical trajectory, pink dashed for projected continuation, background zone tinting for risk levels (pink zone = high, orange zone = medium). When the trajectory crosses into a higher zone, it triggers visual alerts. Enables proactive SC intervention before crises.

Trend Analysis — Rolling Average + Regression
Best for → Smoothing noisy data · Direction detection · Performance trending
Three layers: dim grey dots/line for raw actual values, solid pink for the rolling average (smoothed), and dashed orange for the linear regression trend line. The raw data shows noise, the rolling average shows recent direction, and the trend line shows long-term trajectory. Essential for SC caseload analysis where individual weeks vary but the trend matters.
